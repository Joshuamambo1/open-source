### English

**Brief Summary (2‑3 sentences)**  
CloudMeet is an open‑source, self‑hosted alternative to Calendly that runs on Cloudflare’s free tier and syncs with Google Calendar and Outlook. Built in TypeScript, it automates meeting‑scheduling workflows, eliminating the manual back‑and‑forth that typically slows down operational tasks. With recent commits, a modest but active community, and solid integration points, it’s ready for pilot‑level deployment.

**Value**  
- **Automation of repetitive scheduling** – eliminates the need for manual email coordination, freeing up time for higher‑value work.  
- **Tool‑agnostic calendar sync** – works with both Google Calendar and Outlook, so teams can keep their existing calendars while gaining a unified booking interface.  
- **Cost‑effective hosting** – runs on Cloudflare’s free tier, removing infrastructure overhead and keeping total cost of ownership low.  

**Practical Adoption Path**  
1. **Proof‑of‑Concept (PoC)** – Clone the repo, follow the README to deploy a test instance on Cloudflare Workers (or a local dev environment). Verify Google/Outlook OAuth flow and basic booking UI.  
2. **Pilot Integration** – Connect a small team’s calendars, configure any required webhook or notification integrations, and run a limited‑scope pilot (e.g., internal meetings or client demos).  
3. **Scale & Harden** – Add custom branding, enable rate‑limiting or Cloudflare Access for security, and write CI/CD pipelines for automated deployments.  
4. **Full Roll‑out** – Replace existing Calendly links with CloudMeet URLs, monitor usage, and iterate on workflow automations (e.g., auto‑creating Zoom links or Slack reminders).  

**Production Readiness**  
- **Activity & Community** – 502 GitHub stars, 56 forks, recent commits (as of 2026‑07‑02), and multiple topics indicate an engaged project.  
- **Technical Stack** – TypeScript codebase is modern and aligns with many organizations’ front‑end/backend skill sets.  
- **Deployment Simplicity** – Designed for Cloudflare’s free tier, requiring minimal ops overhead.  
- **Risks** – License compliance, security audit, and maintainer responsiveness need a final check, but no major metadata concerns were found. Overall, CloudMeet meets the criteria for a serious pilot and can be considered production‑ready for organizations comfortable with a small open‑source dependency.
