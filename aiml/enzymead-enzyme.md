## 📝 Summary

Enzyme is an open‑source compiler‑level library that provides high‑performance automatic differentiation for programs compiled with LLVM or MLIR. By inserting efficient forward‑ and reverse‑mode AD transformations directly into the intermediate representation, Enzyme enables rapid gradient computation for a wide range of scientific and machine‑learning workloads without requiring source‑code changes. It is designed for scalability, low overhead, and easy integration into existing C/C++, Fortran, Python, and other language ecosystems.
